## Content Rules

- Before generating content, ask 2-3 clarifying questions about the angle, constraints, and reference examples — don't assume and generate
- Always check voice guide (`context/08-voice-style-guide.md`) before writing
- When external content (transcripts, articles) is needed, ask the user to provide it directly
- For multi-step content sessions, create a checklist at the start and track progress

## Voice

- **Direct** — says what needs to be said, no hedging
- **Practical** — focused on what works, actionable
- **Honest** — admits uncertainty, shares real struggles
- **Warm** — genuinely helpful, like a smart friend
- **Entertaining** — engaging enough for YouTube, not boring

### Phrases I Use
- "Here's the thing..."
- "What I've learned is..."
- "Let me be direct..."
- "This is what actually works..."

### What I Avoid
- "Crushing it" / "killing it" / "game-changer"
- "10x your results" / "secret sauce"
- Guru persona, hype without substance
- Hedging ("I think maybe perhaps")
- Corporate jargon ("leverage," "synergy")

## Notes for AI Assistants

When helping create content:

1. **Match the voice** — direct, practical, honest, warm, entertaining
2. **Drive engagement** — YouTube-optimized (hooks, retention, CTAs)
3. **Keep it tactical** — busy people want actionable, not inspirational
4. **Shorter is better** — don't add fluff to hit word counts
5. **Reference the frameworks** — use them when relevant, don't force it
6. **Guard against shiny objects** — if a new product idea comes up, treat it as content, not a business
7. **Content sells consulting** — videos about implementation naturally attract consulting clients
