The best salespeople don't wing it. They walk into every call knowing the company, the stakeholders, and the angles. Claude turns a 15-minute research sprint into a complete pre-call brief that makes you sound like you've been following the prospect for months.

## Prompts by Call Type

Different calls need different prep. Here are specialized prompts for the four most common sales call types.

### Discovery Call Prep

The goal is to ask smart questions, not pitch. Your prep should focus on hypotheses to validate.

<PromptBlock
  title="Discovery call preparation"
  prompt="I have a discovery call with [name], [title] at [company]. Three hypotheses about their situation I should validate on this call\n2. For each hypothesis, give me 2 discovery questions — one open-ended, one specific\n3. A natural way to open the call that references something specific about them\n4. The ONE thing I should learn by the end of this call to know if they're a real opportunity\n5. A suggested next step to propose if the call goes well\n\nKeep this practical. I want to sound informed, not scripted."

### Demo Call Prep

They've already shown interest. Now you need to show them exactly how you solve their specific problem.

<PromptBlock
  title="Demo call preparation"
  prompt="I have a demo call with [name] at [company]. Here's what I learned from our discovery call:\n\n[Paste your discovery call notes here]\n\nBased on these notes, help me prepare:\n1. The 3 features/capabilities I should demo, ranked by their relevance to this prospect's specific pain points\n2. For each feature, a transition phrase that connects it to something they said in discovery\n3. A talk track for the opening 2 minutes that recaps what I heard and sets up why I'm showing what I'm showing\n4. Two customer stories I should reference (give me the structure — I'll fill in real names): industry-relevant wins that mirror their situation\n5. Anticipated questions they'll ask during the demo and how to answer them\n6. The ask at the end: what's the natural next step based on where they are in their buying process?"

## The Pre-Call Research Project

If you run a lot of sales calls, set up a Claude Project with your company context loaded in. This way you don't have to re-explain who you are and what you sell every time.

<StepByStep
  title="Set up your sales prep Project"
  steps={[
    { title: "Create a new Project called 'Sales Prep'", description: "In Claude, go to Projects and create a new one. Name it something like 'Sales Call Prep' or '[Company] Sales Research'." },
    { title: "Add your company context", description: "Upload or paste: your product overview, ICP description, pricing tiers, top 3 case studies, common objections and responses, and your sales process stages." },
    { title: "Add a custom instruction", description: "In the Project instructions, write: 'You are helping me prepare for sales calls. I sell [product] to [ICP]. When I give you a prospect's info, generate a complete pre-call brief. Be specific and actionable, not generic.'" },
    { title: "Use it before every call", description: "Open the Project, paste the prospect's details, and get a tailored brief in under 2 minutes. The context carries over, so Claude already knows your product, pricing, and differentiators." }
  ]}
/>

<TipCallout type="note">
Projects keep your company context persistent across conversations. Instead of re-explaining your product every time, you just paste the prospect's info and get a tailored brief. This cuts your prep time from 15 minutes to 3.
</TipCallout>

## Common Mistakes

<ScenarioCard
  scenario="You paste a prospect's LinkedIn URL into Claude and ask it to research them."
  answer="Claude can't browse the web. Instead, copy-paste the actual text from their LinkedIn profile, company website, or any relevant articles. Raw text input always beats asking Claude to fetch something it can't access."
  prompt="Here's the LinkedIn profile text for my next call. Analyze this person's likely priorities and concerns based on their role, experience, and recent activity: [paste profile text]"
/>

<ScenarioCard
  scenario="You use the same generic pre-call prompt for a $5K deal and a $500K enterprise deal."
  answer="Scale matters. For smaller deals, use the All-In-One Research Prompt. For enterprise deals, use the Executive Meeting Prep prompt and layer in org chart analysis, multiple stakeholder profiles, and a formal business case. Match your prep depth to the deal size."
/>

<ScenarioCard
  scenario="You get a great pre-call brief from Claude but never update it after the actual conversation."
  answer="The real value compounds when you feed call outcomes back into Claude. After every call, run the Post-Call Debrief prompt. Over a multi-touch deal, Claude builds an increasingly accurate picture of the prospect's needs, objections, and buying process."
